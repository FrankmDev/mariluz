# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Spanish homeowners and small business owners receiving confusing electricity bills. They are skeptical of energy salespeople, value transparency, and want honest advice before committing to any change. Most browse on mobile, often referred by word-of-mouth. Primary job: "Is my bill too high, and can someone explain it to me clearly?"

## Product Purpose

A trust-building landing page that converts visitors into bill-review consultations. Success is a WhatsApp message or contact form submission with a bill attached. The page must communicate that the review is free, honest, and without obligation. The advisor (Mariluz Marín) is the face of the service; the brand is personal, not institutional.

## Brand Personality

Clear, trustworthy, approachable, warm. Three words: **honest, transparent, human**. The Apple Liquid Glass aesthetic signals modern, premium, and transparent — like looking through clear glass. Not pushy, not corporate, not a comparison tool.

## Anti-references

- Corporate/consulting clichés: stock photos of handshakes, generic "professional" blue templates, aggressive sales tactics
- Typical Spanish energy comparison sites: aggressive pop-ups, "URGENTE" banners, fake scarcity
- Minimalist editorial magazine aesthetics: display serif + ruled separators + broadsheet grid — wrong register for a personal service
- AI-generated SaaS default: beige/cream backgrounds, tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section, identical card grids

## Design Principles

1. **Transparency as texture** — the glass metaphor is literal; nothing is hidden, the design feels open and honest
2. **Warmth over corporate cold** — the advisor is a person, not a company; the interface should feel human and approachable
3. **Clarity before persuasion** — explain the service first, ask for contact second; no dark patterns
4. **Mobile-first confidence** — most visitors browse on phones; the design must feel native and effortless, not cramped
5. **Honesty as a differentiator** — the "no saving = no change" message is central, not a footnote; trust is the product

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target
- Spanish language throughout; all user-facing text in Spanish
- Reduced motion: already implemented in CSS (`prefers-reduced-motion`)
- Reduced transparency: already implemented in CSS (`prefers-reduced-transparency`)
- High contrast: already implemented in CSS (`prefers-contrast: high`)
- Focus states: visible focus rings on all interactive elements
